# Gear & Maintenance

## Essential Kit for Wedding Photography

### Camera Bodies
- **Two bodies minimum** for weddings. If one fails, you're covered.
- Entry: Sony A7 IV, Canon R6 III, Nikon Z6 III (~$2,000-2,500)
- Mid: Sony A7R V, Canon R5 II, Nikon Z8 (~$3,000-4,000)
- Pro: Sony A1, Canon R3, Nikon Z9 (~$5,000-6,500)
- Dual card slots are non-negotiable for weddings.

### Lenses — The Core Three
- **24-70mm f/2.8** — Workhorse. Ceremony, groups, reception, details.
- **70-200mm f/2.8** — Ceremony (from the back), candids, compressed portraits.
- **35mm or 50mm f/1.4** — Low light, getting ready, environmental portraits, dancing.
- Add an **85mm f/1.4** if you love portrait compression and bokeh.

### Flash
- **Godox AD200 Pro** — Versatile, powerful, affordable. Fits in a light stand bracket or on-camera.
- **Godox V1 or V860 III** — On-camera with round head for better bounce.
- **MagMod modifiers** — MagSphere for on-camera diffusion, MagGrid for reception hair lights.
- Carry 2 flashes minimum. Backup matters.

### Memory Cards
- **UHS-II SD** minimum for mirrorless (V60 or V90).
- **CFexpress Type B** for high-burst cameras (Z8, R5 II).
- Dual card strategy: mirror (same files on both cards) for weddings.
- Carry more cards than you think you need.

### Batteries
- 4-6 batteries per body for a full wedding day.
- Stick with OEM batteries for reliability. Third-party can have firmware lockouts.
- Charge all batteries the night before. Mark charged vs. dead (rubber band method).

## Maintenance Schedule

| Task | Frequency |
|------|-----------|
| Sensor cleaning (DIY with swabs + solution) | Every 2-3 months |
| Professional sensor cleaning | Every 6-12 months |
| Lens firmware updates | Check quarterly |
| Camera firmware updates | When released — read changelog first |
| Check lens contacts for corrosion | Monthly |
| Clean lens glass (front + rear elements) | Before each shoot |
| Inspect flash heads and modifiers | Monthly |
| Test all gear in a dry run | Before any major shoot |

## Packing Lists by Shoot Type

### Full Wedding Day
- 2 camera bodies, 3-4 lenses (24-70, 70-200, 35, 85)
- 2-3 flashes, light stand, modifier
- 8+ memory cards, 6+ batteries per body
- Gaffer tape, lens cloths, Allen wrench set
- Snacks, water, phone charger
- Business cards, emergency kit (safety pins, stain remover, breath mints)
- Rain cover for gear if outdoor

### Portrait Session (1-2 hours)
- 1-2 bodies, 2 lenses (85mm + 35mm or 24-70)
- 1 flash + modifier (if needed)
- 4 memory cards, 2-3 batteries
- Reflector (5-in-1 collapsible)

### Elopement / Adventure
- 1 body + 1 backup (smaller body is fine)
- 2 compact lenses (35mm + 85mm, or 24-70 only)
- 1 small flash (Godox V1)
- Minimize weight — you're hiking. Every ounce counts.
- Weatherproof bag, lens cloths, rain sleeve

## Backup Philosophy
- **Dual card slots = your first backup.** Mirror mode, not overflow.
- **Ingest immediately** when you get home. Copy to primary drive AND backup drive before touching Lightroom.
- **3-2-1 rule:** 3 copies, 2 different media, 1 offsite (cloud or separate location).
- Keep raws for at least 1 year post-delivery. Communicate retention policy to clients.
