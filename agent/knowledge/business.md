# Photography Business

## Pricing Strategy
Don't price based on what competitors charge. Calculate your **Cost of Doing Business (CODB):**

1. Annual expenses: gear depreciation, insurance, software, education, marketing, website, travel, taxes (~30% of revenue)
2. Desired salary: what you want to take home
3. Expected bookings: realistic number per year
4. **(Expenses + Salary) / Bookings = Minimum per booking**

If that number scares you, you're either undercharging or need more volume.

## Package Structures
Common tiers:
- **Elopement:** 2-4 hours, 1 photographer, digital gallery. $1,500-3,000.
- **Half Day:** 4-6 hours, 1 photographer, digital gallery. $2,500-4,500.
- **Full Day:** 8-10 hours, 1-2 photographers, digital gallery + engagement session. $4,000-7,000.
- **Premium:** Full day + album + prints + second shooter + extra hours. $6,000-10,000+.

Always include: number of hours, number of photographers, estimated image count, delivery timeline, usage rights.

## Contracts — Essential Clauses
- **Cancellation/rescheduling:** Retainer is non-refundable. Rescheduling within 12 months at no extra charge.
- **Image rights:** Photographer retains copyright. Client gets personal use license. Specify social media, print, commercial limits.
- **Liability:** Photographer not liable for missed shots due to venue restrictions, weather, or guest interference. Uncle Bob clause.
- **Force majeure:** Covers both parties for acts of God, pandemics, venue cancellations.
- **Payment schedule:** Retainer (25-50%) to book, balance due 30 days before event.
- **Delivery timeline:** Sneak peeks within 48 hours, full gallery within 6-8 weeks.
- **Model release:** Permission to use images for portfolio, social media, marketing.

## Client Communication Templates
- **Inquiry reply:** Respond within 24 hours. Acknowledge their event, share your availability, attach pricing guide, suggest a call.
- **Booking confirmation:** Thank them, confirm date + package, attach contract, include payment link.
- **Pre-event questionnaire:** Send 4-6 weeks out. Ask for timeline, family formal list, must-have shots, vendor contacts, special requests.
- **Gallery delivery:** Warm, personal email. Link to gallery. Mention print options. Ask for a review.
- **Review request:** Send 1-2 weeks after delivery. Direct link to Google or The Knot review page.
- **Anniversary follow-up:** One year later. Quick note, maybe a favorite image. Plants the seed for referrals.

## Deposits & Payments
- Industry standard: 25-50% retainer to secure the date.
- Balance due 30 days before the event (not day-of — you don't want payment stress on their wedding day).
- Clearly state: retainer is non-refundable. It holds the date and turns away other bookings.

## Insurance
- **General liability ($1-2M):** Required by most venues. Covers property damage, injury claims.
- **Equipment coverage:** Replacement value for stolen/damaged gear. ~$300-500/year for $15-20K in gear.
- **Errors & omissions:** Covers claims of missed deliverables or dissatisfaction. Peace of mind.
- Providers: Hill & Usher, Full Frame Insurance, PPA membership includes some coverage.

## Taxes
- Set aside 25-30% of revenue for taxes (income + self-employment).
- Pay quarterly estimated taxes to avoid penalties.
- Deductible: gear, mileage (keep a log), software subscriptions, education/workshops, home office, second shooter fees, marketing costs.
- LLC vs. sole proprietorship: LLC provides liability protection. Talk to an accountant.
