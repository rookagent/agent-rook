# Post-Processing Workflow

## Culling Strategy
1. **Import** with metadata applied (copyright, keywords, date).
2. **First pass:** Flag picks (P) and rejects (X). Go fast — gut reactions. Don't pixel-peep.
3. **Second pass:** Star-rate the picks (3-5 stars). 3 = good, 4 = great, 5 = portfolio/hero.
4. **Target:** Deliver 50-80 images per hour of coverage. 8-hour wedding = 400-650 images.
5. **Tools:** Photo Mechanic for speed culling, Aftershoot AI for auto-culling.

## Lightroom Classic Workflow
1. **Import** → apply a base preset (exposure, contrast, tone curve baseline).
2. **White balance** first — get it right, everything else follows.
3. **Exposure, highlights, shadows** — recover, don't overdo. Flat HDR look = amateur hour.
4. **Tone curve** for mood — lift the blacks slightly for film feel, S-curve for contrast.
5. **HSL** — fine-tune skin tones (orange and yellow luminance), desaturate distracting colors.
6. **Sync settings** across similar shots (same location, same light = batch edit).
7. **Spot-check** — scroll through the full gallery at export size. Catch white balance shifts between locations.

## Color Grading Styles
- **Light and airy:** Lifted shadows, desaturated slightly, bright skin tones. Popular for spring/summer weddings.
- **Dark and moody:** Crushed blacks, warm highlights, cooler shadows. Popular for fall/winter, dramatic venues.
- **Film emulation:** Kodak Portra warmth, slight grain, lifted blacks, muted greens. Timeless look.
- **True to life:** Clean, accurate color. No heavy grading. Lets the moment speak.
- **Consistency matters more than style.** A gallery should feel cohesive even if shot across 5 locations with different lighting.

## Photoshop — When You Need It
- **Frequency separation:** Skin retouching without losing texture. Use sparingly.
- **Sky replacement:** Blown-out sky on an otherwise great shot. Generative fill works surprisingly well.
- **Object removal:** Exit signs, trash cans, Uncle Bob's iPad. Clone stamp or content-aware fill.
- **Head swaps:** Someone blinked in the only group shot. Happens every wedding.
- **Composites:** When you promised the couple a dramatic sky and nature didn't deliver.

## AI-Assisted Editing
- **Aftershoot / Imagen AI:** Train on your editing style, then let AI do the first pass. You fine-tune.
- Works well for: exposure, white balance, tone curve, basic HSL.
- Still needs human eye for: skin retouching, creative grading, album hero shots.
- Can cut editing time by 50-70% on a wedding gallery.

## Delivery
- **Sneak peeks:** 10-20 images within 24-48 hours. Post on social (with permission), send to couple. This is your marketing moment.
- **Full gallery:** 4-8 weeks for weddings, 2-3 weeks for portraits.
- **Online galleries:** Pic-Time, Pixieset, ShootProof, CloudSpot. Include print ordering.
- **Export settings:** Full resolution JPEG, sRGB color space, 85-95% quality. Long edge 4500-6000px.

## Album Design
- **Tools:** Fundy Designer, Smart Albums, or gallery-integrated designers.
- **Spread principles:** Vary layouts (full bleed, grid, single image). Tell a story chronologically.
- **Image count:** 60-100 images for a 30-40 page album.
- **Send a draft** for approval before printing. Allow 2 rounds of revisions.
