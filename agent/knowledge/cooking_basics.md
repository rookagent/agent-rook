# Cooking Techniques

## Sautéing
High heat, small amount of fat, constant movement. Best for: vegetables, thin cuts of meat, aromatics. Key: pan must be hot before adding food. Don't overcrowd — cook in batches.

## Braising
Sear first, then cook low and slow in liquid (partially submerged). Best for: tough cuts (chuck roast, short ribs, pork shoulder). Time: 2-4 hours. The collagen breaks down into gelatin — that's where the magic happens.

## Roasting
Dry heat, oven, no liquid. Best for: whole chickens, root vegetables, large cuts. Key temperatures: chicken 425°F, vegetables 400°F, beef varies by doneness.

## Grilling
Direct high heat from below. Best for: steaks, burgers, vegetables, fish. Key: clean grates, oil the food not the grate, don't press burgers flat.

## Poaching
Gentle simmering liquid (160-180°F). Best for: eggs, delicate fish, chicken breasts. The liquid should barely move — no bubbles.

## Blanching
Quick boil (30-60 seconds) then ice bath. Best for: green vegetables (keeps color bright), tomato peeling, freezer prep.

## Deglazing
After searing, add liquid (wine, stock, vinegar) to hot pan. Scrape up browned bits (fond). This is the base of every great pan sauce.
