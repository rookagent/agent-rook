# Newborn & Baby Photography

## Newborn Sessions (5-14 days old)

### Safety First
- **Never force a pose.** If the baby resists, move to the next setup.
- **Always have a spotter.** Parent's hand within reach for every posed shot — clone it out in post.
- **Support the head.** Newborns can't hold their heads up. Every composite head-on-hands shot is exactly that — a composite.
- **Room temperature:** 78-82°F. Babies sleep better warm. Space heater on standby.
- **White noise machine.** Mimics the womb. Keeps baby asleep through outfit changes and repositioning.
- **Wash your hands.** Before and during. No exceptions.

### Timing
- Book during pregnancy. Shoot between days 5-14 when baby is sleepiest and most curl-able.
- Session length: 2-4 hours. Don't rush it. Feedings, diaper changes, and soothing happen.
- Best time: morning. Babies tend to sleep deeper in the morning.

### Posing Basics
- **Back pose (taco/bum up):** Safest starting pose. Baby on tummy, knees tucked, bum up.
- **Side lying:** Curled on side, hands under cheek.
- **Swaddled/wrapped:** Easiest for fussy babies. Stretchy wraps in neutral colors.
- **Parent poses:** Baby in mom's or dad's hands, on chest, over shoulder. These are the emotional ones.
- **Sibling shots:** Have the older child sit still, place baby in their lap. Spotter ready. Keep it quick.

### Props & Styling
- Less is more. Neutral wraps, a simple basket or bowl, a textured blanket.
- Avoid over-propping. The baby is the subject, not the stuff.
- Fur/flokati blankets photograph well and are easy to work with.
- Headbands and bows: ask the parents' preference. Some love them, some don't.

### Pricing
- Newborn sessions: $300-800 (includes 2-3 hour session, 20-40 edited images).
- **Baby plans** (newborn + 3mo + 6mo + 1yr): $800-2,000. Great recurring revenue.

## Milestone Sessions

### 3-Month (100 Days)
- Baby can't sit yet. Tummy time poses, propped on a pillow, in parent's arms.
- Short session — 30-45 min. They tire fast.
- Lifestyle approach works well: on the bed, in the nursery, with parents.

### 6-Month (Sitter Session)
- Baby can sit unassisted (usually). This opens up a lot of poses.
- Chair, crate, or floor setup. Keep them contained.
- Personality is emerging — big smiles, grabbing things, babbling.
- Bubbles, crinkle toys, peek-a-boo for expressions.

### 9-Month
- Crawling, pulling up, standing with support.
- Capture movement. Let them explore.
- Outdoor sessions work well at this age.

### First Birthday / Cake Smash
- **Setup:** Backdrop + floor drop + cake + outfit. Coordinate colors.
- **Phase 1:** Clean portraits BEFORE the cake comes out. Once it's out, it's over.
- **Phase 2:** Cake smash. Let them go. Capture the mess, the expressions, the frosting hands.
- **Phase 3:** Optional bath splash after. Bucket or tub with warm water, bubbles.
- **Cake:** Ask about allergies. Some parents bring a smash cake from a bakery, some want you to recommend one.
- Session length: 45-60 minutes total.

## Maternity Sessions
- **Timing:** 28-34 weeks. Bump is visible but mom is still comfortable.
- **Wardrobe:** Flowy dresses, fitted tops that show the silhouette. Suggest 2 outfits.
- **Poses:** Hands on belly (both parents), profile shot, backlit silhouette, partner from behind with arms around bump.
- **Location:** Golden hour field, studio with draped fabric, their home (nursery setup).
- **Duration:** 30-45 minutes.

## General Baby Photography Tips
- Let parents soothe. Don't take the baby from them unless they offer.
- Shoot at baby's level, not from above (unless intentional).
- Continuous shooting mode. Babies move fast between expressions.
- Keep sessions shorter than you think. Overtired = meltdown = session over.
- Have a backup plan. If baby won't sleep (newborn) or won't stop crying (any age), switch to parent/family shots and come back.
