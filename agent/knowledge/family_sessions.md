# Family Sessions

## Session Planning
- **Duration:** 30-60 minutes. Longer isn't better — kids have a window and it closes fast.
- **Time of day:** Golden hour (1 hour before sunset) is ideal. Morning sessions work for young kids who nap in the afternoon.
- **Location:** Parks, fields, urban alleys, the family's home (lifestyle). Scout for open shade, good backdrops, and room to run.
- **What to wear:** Coordinated, not matching. Suggest a color palette (neutrals + one accent color). Avoid logos, neon, and busy patterns. Send a style guide.
- **Props:** Keep it minimal. A blanket, a favorite toy, flowers. The family IS the subject.

## Shot List — Standard Family Session
1. Full family together (3-4 variations: standing, walking, sitting, candid)
2. Parents together (couple shots — they rarely get photos without kids)
3. Each parent with each child individually
4. Siblings together
5. Individual kids
6. Candid play — running, tickling, piggyback rides, throwing leaves
7. Detail shots — hands holding, bare feet, family pet if present

## Working with Kids by Age

### Babies (0-12 months)
- Shoot around their schedule (fed, rested, dry).
- Parent is the posing tool — baby in arms, over shoulder, on hip.
- Get down low. Shoot at their level.
- Rattles, crinkle toys, peek-a-boo to get eye contact.

### Toddlers (1-3 years)
- Do NOT try to pose them. Chase them with a long lens.
- Give them a task: "Run to daddy!" "Show me how you jump!" "Pick that flower!"
- Burst mode. Pre-focus where they'll end up.
- Have a helper (parent, assistant) behind you making faces.
- The best toddler shots happen in the first 10 minutes. Front-load the must-haves.

### Kids (4-8 years)
- They can follow simple directions. "Stand right here and look at me."
- Make it fun — silly faces, "who can jump highest?", whisper games.
- Bribery is fine. Tell parents to bring a treat for after.
- Genuine laughs > forced smiles. Tell a bad joke.

### Tweens/Teens (9-17)
- They're self-conscious. Don't over-direct.
- Give them something to lean on, sit on, or interact with.
- Individual portraits matter more at this age — they want to look cool.
- Music helps. Let them pick a playlist.

## Extended Family Sessions
- Get the big group shot first while everyone's fresh and cooperative.
- Then: each nuclear family, grandparents with all grandkids, grandparents alone, cousins together.
- Assign a wrangler from the family (usually an aunt — there's always one).
- Use terrain: porch steps, a hillside, varied seating heights.
- Expect 45-75 minutes for a large group (12+ people).

## Pricing for Family Sessions
- Mini session (20 min, limited gallery): $150-350
- Standard session (45-60 min, full gallery): $300-600
- Extended family (60-90 min, multiple groupings): $500-1,000+
- Prices vary wildly by market. Know your CODB.

## Seasonal Considerations
- **Fall:** Peak family photo season. Book early, golden light through colored leaves. Fields, farms, pumpkin patches.
- **Spring:** Blooming trees, wildflowers. Watch for allergies and wind.
- **Summer:** Shoot early morning or late evening. Avoid harsh midday. Beach and water sessions are popular.
- **Winter:** Holiday cards drive bookings. Studio, snowy outdoors, or cozy indoor lifestyle. Short sessions — it's cold.
