# Headshots & Personal Branding

## Types of Headshot Sessions

### Corporate / LinkedIn Headshots
- **Goal:** Professional, approachable, trustworthy.
- **Background:** Clean — solid gray, white, or blurred office environment.
- **Lighting:** Clamshell or butterfly for even, flattering light. No dramatic shadows.
- **Wardrobe:** Solid colors, no patterns. Dark suits/blazers photograph best. Avoid white (blows out near the face).
- **Expression:** Confident smile, slight head tilt. Not stiff — "think about something that makes you proud."
- **Crop:** Head and shoulders. Leave space above the head. Centered or slight rule-of-thirds.
- **Delivery:** Both horizontal and vertical crops. Clients need both for different platforms.

### Personal Branding Sessions
- **Goal:** Tell the client's professional story. Not just a headshot — lifestyle + personality + environment.
- **Duration:** 1-2 hours. Multiple outfits, locations, and scenarios.
- **Shots to include:**
  - Classic headshot (the anchor image)
  - Working shots (at desk, on laptop, with product, in their space)
  - Environmental (walking, coffee shop, outdoor — shows their world)
  - Detail shots (hands typing, tools of their trade, branded items)
  - Casual/approachable (laughing, candid, mid-conversation)
- **Planning:** Send a questionnaire: What's your brand? Who's your audience? Where will you use these? What do you want people to feel when they see your photo?
- **Deliverables:** 30-60 images. Mix of horizontal and vertical for website, social, press.

### Actor / Creative Headshots
- **Goal:** Capture range and authenticity. Casting directors want to see the real person.
- **Looks:** 2-3 outfit changes, different moods (commercial smile, theatrical intensity, casual).
- **Background:** Simple, not distracting. Blurred outdoor or studio.
- **Lighting:** Natural or one-light setup. Nothing over-produced.
- **Eyes are everything.** Sharp, connected, telling a story.
- **Crop:** Tight on the face. No full-body for headshots.

### Team / Company Photos
- **Logistics:** Book enough time. 5-10 minutes per person for individuals. 15-20 for group.
- **Consistency:** Same backdrop, same lighting, same crop. Everyone should look like they belong together.
- **Setup:** Bring the studio to them (portable backdrop, 2-light setup). Or use a clean wall/conference room.
- **Group shot:** Arrange by height, stagger rows. CEO/leadership in front. Keep it natural, not yearbook-stiff.
- **Turnaround:** Companies want fast delivery. 3-5 business days.

## Retouching for Headshots
- **Always:** Blemish removal (temporary imperfections), under-eye reduction, teeth whitening (subtle), stray hair cleanup.
- **Sometimes:** Skin smoothing (frequency separation — light touch), wrinkle reduction (reduce, don't remove), background cleanup.
- **Never:** Make them look like a different person. They need to be recognizable in a meeting.
- **Communication:** "I'll clean up temporary blemishes. I won't change your face. You'll look like you on your best day."

## Tips for Better Headshots
- **Tethering:** Shoot tethered to a laptop so the subject can see themselves. Builds confidence and speeds up posing.
- **Talk to them.** Silence = tension. Ask about their work, their weekend, their dog. Real conversation = natural expressions.
- **Music.** Play something upbeat. Sets the mood.
- **Take breaks.** After 10 minutes of posing, people tighten up. Step back, let them shake it off, restart.
- **Jaw to shoulder.** "Turn your body slightly, bring your chin toward your front shoulder." Instant dimension.
