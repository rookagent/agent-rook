# Wedding Day Workflow

## Typical Timeline (8-hour day with first look)

| Time | Phase | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| 2:00 PM | Arrive — getting ready | Bride's room first (details, dress, hair/makeup finishing) |
| 2:30 PM | Groom getting ready | 20 min — suit, cufflinks, boutonniere, candids with groomsmen |
| 3:00 PM | First look | Private, emotional. Position couple, step back. 10-15 min. |
| 3:15 PM | Couple portraits — round 1 | 30 min at first location. Get the safe shots first. |
| 3:45 PM | Bridal party photos | Full group, then sides. Keep it moving — 20 min max. |
| 4:15 PM | Family formals | Pre-made list. Largest group first, dismiss as you go. 20 min. |
| 4:45 PM | Buffer / travel | Always build in 15-30 min. Something always runs late. |
| 5:00 PM | Ceremony | Arrive early. Scout positions. Silent shutter on. |
| 5:30 PM | Cocktail hour | Detail shots (centerpieces, cake, venue), candids, vendor shots. |
| 6:30 PM | Reception — intros, first dance, toasts | Don't miss any of these. Two angles if you have a second shooter. |
| 7:00 PM | Dinner | Eat your vendor meal. Shoot candids between courses. |
| 7:30 PM | Parent dances, cake, bouquet | Back-to-back reception events. Stay ready. |
| 7:45 PM | Golden hour portraits — round 2 | Pull the couple for 15-20 min. This is often where the magic shots happen. |
| 8:00 PM | Open dancing | Dance floor shots, candids, sparkler/exit if applicable. |

## No First Look — What Changes
- Couple portraits move to after the ceremony (cocktail hour or sunset).
- Family formals happen right after ceremony — more rushed.
- You lose 60-90 minutes of portrait time. Plan accordingly.
- Getting ready coverage starts earlier.

## Getting Ready Must-Haves
- Dress hanging (backlit window if possible)
- Detail flat lay (rings, invitation, shoes, perfume, jewelry)
- Hair/makeup finishing — candids, not posed
- Putting on the dress (zip or button moment)
- Bridesmaids' reaction to seeing the bride
- Groom: jacket on, tie, cufflinks, reading a letter (if applicable)

## Family Formals — Running Them Fast
1. Get the list in advance (ask the couple to fill out a questionnaire).
2. Start with the largest group (all family) — dismiss as you go.
3. Have the couple's point person wrangle people, not you.
4. Shoot 3-5 frames per grouping. Move on.
5. 20 minutes for 8-12 groupings is tight but doable.
6. Never say "one more" — say "got it, thank you!"

## Working with Vendors
- **Videographer:** Coordinate first look positioning. Alternate ceremony angles. Don't walk through their establishing shots.
- **Planner/coordinator:** Get the timeline in advance. Confirm golden hour window. Ask about ceremony restrictions.
- **DJ:** Confirm reception event order. Ask for mic announcements if you need the couple for portraits.
- **Vendor meals:** Eat when they say eat. You won't get another chance.

## Key Moments to Never Miss
- First look reaction (or aisle reaction if no first look)
- Ring exchange
- First kiss
- Recessional (the walk back — pure joy)
- First dance
- Parent toasts (both the speaker AND the couple's reactions)
- Cake cut
- Exit/send-off
