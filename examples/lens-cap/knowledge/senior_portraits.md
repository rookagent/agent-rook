# Senior Portraits

## The Market
- Every high school junior/senior needs portraits. It's a reliable, annual market.
- Booking window: spring of junior year through fall of senior year.
- Seniors (and their parents) share heavily on social. Great referral engine.
- Many schools no longer require a specific studio — the market is open to independents.

## Session Planning
- **Duration:** 60-90 minutes. Enough for 2-4 outfit changes and 2-3 locations.
- **Time:** Golden hour. Always. Teens look great in warm light.
- **Location:** Urban downtown, graffiti walls, parks, train tracks (be safe and legal), their school campus, sports fields, a family farm. Variety matters — different backdrops for different outfits.
- **Wardrobe:** 2-4 outfits. Suggest: one dressy, one casual, one that represents "them" (sport uniform, dance costume, band instrument, art supplies). Send a style guide in advance.
- **Props:** Letterman jacket, sports equipment, musical instrument, pet, car. Whatever defines them.

## Posing Teens
- **Don't over-pose.** Teens can smell fake from a mile away.
- **Movement-based:** Walking, leaning, sitting on steps, looking over their shoulder.
- **Give them something to do with their hands:** Pockets, holding jacket over shoulder, fixing hair, holding a prop.
- **Lean against things:** Walls, trees, cars, railings. Creates natural angles.
- **Candid moments:** Laughing at a joke, mid-walk, looking away. These are often their favorites.
- **Mirror their energy.** Quiet kid? Keep it chill. Outgoing kid? Hype them up.
- **Music.** Let THEM pick the playlist. Instant vibe shift.

## Shot List
1. Classic headshot (yearbook-ready, clean background)
2. Full-length portrait (shows outfit, environment)
3. Environmental/location shot (urban, nature, school)
4. Activity/passion shot (sport, instrument, art, dance)
5. Cap and gown (if applicable — some save this for graduation day)
6. With friends (if they bring them — great for social sharing)
7. Candid/personality shots (laughing, movement, real expressions)

## Working with Parents
- **They're paying.** Communicate with them about logistics, pricing, and delivery.
- **They want different shots than the teen.** Parents want the classic, clean portrait. Teens want the edgy/creative ones. Shoot both.
- **Gallery proofing:** Let the family choose together. Offer a viewing session or online gallery with favorites.
- **Upsell:** Graduation announcements, canvas wraps, wallet prints for grandma. Products sell well in this market.

## Pricing
- Standard session (60-90 min, 30-50 images): $250-500
- Premium (90 min, 50-75 images + prints credit): $400-800
- Mini/headshot only (20 min, 5-10 images): $100-200
- Add-on: graduation announcements, wallet prints, yearbook package

## Marketing to Seniors
- **Senior reps/ambassadors:** Offer a free or discounted session to popular juniors in exchange for social sharing and referrals. They bring their friends.
- **Instagram is your portfolio.** Post senior work consistently during booking season (Jan-Sep).
- **Partner with local businesses:** Prom dress shops, tux rentals, salons — cross-promote.
- **Hashtag:** #[YourCity]SeniorPhotographer, #ClassOf2027, #SeniorSzn
- **Yearbook deadlines:** Know them. Offer "yearbook-ready" packages with specific crop and format requirements.

## Seasonal Timing
- **Spring (Mar-May):** Junior year sessions, early birds, blooming backgrounds.
- **Summer (Jun-Aug):** Peak season. Warm light, flexible schedules, best variety of locations.
- **Fall (Sep-Nov):** Fall colors, football jerseys, last-chance sessions before yearbook deadlines.
- **Winter:** Cap and gown season for December graduates. Indoor/studio options.
