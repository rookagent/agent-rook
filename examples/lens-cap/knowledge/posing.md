# Posing Guide

## Couples — 5 Base Poses
1. **V-shape:** Facing each other at 45 degrees, bodies form a V. Foreheads close. Intimate.
2. **Closed embrace:** Chest to chest, arms wrapped. One person's chin on the other's shoulder.
3. **Walking:** Hand in hand, moving toward camera. Natural, relaxed. Tell them to actually walk — not pose-walk.
4. **Forehead touch:** Noses almost touching, eyes closed. Quiet, emotional.
5. **The dip:** One partner leans the other back. Dramatic, fun. Save for when they're warmed up.

From these 5, you get 20+ variations by changing hand placement, eye direction (at each other, at camera, eyes closed), and adding movement.

## Prompts Over Poses (Modern Directing)
Instead of placing hands and adjusting chins, give prompts:
- "Walk toward me and tell each other your favorite thing about today."
- "Whisper something that'll make them laugh."
- "Look at each other like you have a secret."
- "Close your eyes and just breathe for a second."
- "One of you pick the other up — doesn't matter who."
- "Slow dance, no music. Ignore me."

This produces genuine expressions. Posed photos look posed. Prompted photos look real.

## Individual Portraits
- **Weight shift:** Weight on the back foot, front knee slightly bent. Instantly more relaxed.
- **Chin forward and down:** Eliminates double chin, defines jawline. "Push your forehead toward me" is easier to understand than "chin down."
- **Hands:** Give them something to do. Pocket, lapel, hair, bouquet. Idle hands look awkward.
- **Angle:** Shoot slightly above eye level for most flattering perspective. Never shoot up into nostrils.

## Bridal Portraits
- Train placement: fan it out, have a bridesmaid hold it, let it trail on stairs.
- Veil: over the face for dramatic, tossed in wind, draped over one shoulder, wrapped around couple.
- Bouquet: generally at waist height, tilted slightly toward camera. Ring hand visible.
- Over-the-shoulder: Bride looking back. Classic, elegant.

## Group Posing
- **4 people:** Diamond formation or staggered line.
- **8 people:** Two rows, stagger heights. Back row on step if available.
- **12+ people:** Use architecture (stairs, benches, terrain). Three rows.
- **20+ people:** Go wide, shoot from elevation if possible. Or embrace the chaos — arrange roughly and shoot a few frames.

## Plus-Size Subjects
- Angle the body 45 degrees. Straight-on is least flattering for anyone.
- Shoot slightly above eye level.
- Flag the light — don't light the full body evenly; let shadow define shape.
- Arms slightly away from body (hand on hip, holding bouquet, one arm on partner).
- None of this should be obvious. Good posing is good posing for everyone.

## Kids and Candids
- Don't try to pose kids under 5. Just chase them with a long lens.
- Give them a job: "Can you show me how fast you can run to that tree?"
- Burst mode. Pre-focus on the spot they'll end up.
- The best kid photos happen when they forget you exist.
