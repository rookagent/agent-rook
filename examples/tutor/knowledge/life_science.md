# Life Science

## Animal Classification (Grades 2-5)

### Vertebrates (Animals WITH Backbones)

**Mammals:**
- Warm-blooded, breathe air, have hair/fur
- Babies born live (mostly), drink mother's milk
- Examples: dogs, cats, whales, bats, humans, elephants
- Special: whales and dolphins are mammals (not fish!), bats are mammals (not birds!)

**Birds:**
- Warm-blooded, feathers, lay eggs, most can fly
- Hollow bones (lighter for flying)
- Examples: eagles, penguins (can't fly!), hummingbirds, ostriches (can't fly!)

**Reptiles:**
- Cold-blooded, scales, most lay eggs
- Breathe air (even sea turtles must surface)
- Examples: snakes, lizards, turtles, crocodiles, alligators

**Amphibians:**
- Cold-blooded, smooth/moist skin, lay eggs in water
- Most live part of life in water, part on land
- Metamorphosis: egg → tadpole → frog
- Examples: frogs, toads, salamanders, newts

**Fish:**
- Cold-blooded, scales, breathe through gills, live in water
- Fins for swimming, most lay eggs
- Examples: salmon, sharks, clownfish, goldfish

### Invertebrates (Animals WITHOUT Backbones)
97% of all animals! Including:
- **Insects:** 6 legs, 3 body parts (head, thorax, abdomen), antennae. Butterflies, ants, beetles.
- **Arachnids:** 8 legs, 2 body parts. Spiders, scorpions, ticks.
- **Crustaceans:** Hard shell, many legs. Crabs, lobsters, shrimp.
- **Mollusks:** Soft bodies, many have shells. Snails, clams, octopus, squid.
- **Worms:** Long, soft bodies. Earthworms, leeches.

## Plants (Grades 2-4)

### What Plants Need
1. **Sunlight** — energy source
2. **Water** — absorbed through roots
3. **Carbon dioxide (CO₂)** — absorbed from air through leaves
4. **Nutrients** — from soil, absorbed through roots
5. **Space** — room to grow

### Photosynthesis (The Big Idea)
Plants make their own food using sunlight:
**Sunlight + Water + CO₂ → Sugar (food) + Oxygen**

The leaves are the food factories. Chlorophyll (the green pigment) captures sunlight.

**Explain it as:** "Plants are like little solar-powered kitchens. They use sunlight to cook water and air into sugar. And their leftovers? Oxygen — the stuff we breathe!"

### Parts of a Plant
- **Roots:** Anchor plant, absorb water and nutrients from soil
- **Stem:** Supports the plant, transports water up and food down (like a highway)
- **Leaves:** Where photosynthesis happens. Has tiny holes (stomata) for gas exchange.
- **Flowers:** Reproductive parts. Attract pollinators with color/smell.
- **Seeds:** Contain a baby plant + food supply. Dispersed by wind, water, animals.
- **Fruit:** Protects seeds. Often tasty to encourage animals to eat and spread seeds.

### Plant Life Cycle
Seed → Germination (sprout) → Seedling → Adult plant → Flower → Pollination → Seeds → Repeat

## Ecosystems and Food Chains (Grades 3-5)

### Ecosystem
A community of living things (biotic) interacting with their non-living environment (abiotic: water, sunlight, soil, temperature).

### Habitats
Where an organism lives. Each habitat has specific conditions:
- **Desert:** Hot, dry, sandy. Cacti, lizards, camels.
- **Forest:** Lots of trees, moderate rain. Deer, bears, owls.
- **Ocean:** Saltwater, vast. Fish, whales, coral.
- **Rainforest:** Hot, very wet. Most biodiversity on Earth. Monkeys, toucans, jaguars.
- **Tundra:** Extremely cold, frozen ground. Polar bears, arctic foxes.
- **Grassland:** Open, flat, grassy. Bison, lions, zebras.

### Food Chains
Shows the flow of energy from one organism to another:
**Sun → Producer → Primary Consumer → Secondary Consumer → Tertiary Consumer**

Example: Sun → Grass → Rabbit → Fox → Eagle

### Roles
- **Producers:** Make their own food (plants, algae). Always at the base.
- **Consumers:** Eat other organisms.
  - Herbivore: eats plants (rabbit, deer, cow)
  - Carnivore: eats animals (lion, hawk, shark)
  - Omnivore: eats both (bear, human, pig)
- **Decomposers:** Break down dead organisms. Fungi, bacteria, worms. Recycle nutrients back to soil.

### Food Web
Real ecosystems have many overlapping food chains = a food web. A rabbit eats many plants. A fox eats rabbits AND mice AND birds.

### Energy Pyramid
Energy decreases at each level. Only about 10% of energy passes from one level to the next.
- Producers: 100% of energy
- Primary consumers: 10%
- Secondary consumers: 1%
- Tertiary consumers: 0.1%

This is why there are way more plants than herbivores, and way more herbivores than predators.

## The Human Body (Grades 3-5)

### Major Organ Systems

**Skeletal System:**
- 206 bones in an adult
- Supports the body, protects organs, allows movement
- Joints: where bones meet (knee, elbow, shoulder)
- Bones are alive — they grow, heal, and make blood cells

**Muscular System:**
- Over 600 muscles
- Skeletal muscles: voluntary (you control them) — walking, lifting
- Smooth muscles: involuntary — stomach, intestines, blood vessels
- Cardiac muscle: involuntary — heart only

**Digestive System:**
Mouth → Esophagus → Stomach → Small intestine → Large intestine → Out
- Mouth: teeth break food down, saliva starts digestion
- Stomach: acid breaks food down further (2-4 hours)
- Small intestine: nutrients absorbed into blood (most digestion happens here!)
- Large intestine: water absorbed, waste formed

**Circulatory System:**
- Heart pumps blood through blood vessels
- Arteries: carry blood AWAY from heart (oxygenated)
- Veins: carry blood back TO heart (deoxygenated)
- Capillaries: tiny vessels where exchange happens (oxygen/nutrients out, CO₂/waste in)
- Heart beats ~100,000 times per day

**Respiratory System:**
- Nose/mouth → trachea → bronchi → lungs → alveoli
- Inhale: oxygen in. Exhale: carbon dioxide out.
- Alveoli = tiny air sacs where oxygen enters blood
- Diaphragm muscle controls breathing

**Nervous System:**
- Brain: control center. Thinking, feeling, remembering, controlling body.
- Spinal cord: information highway between brain and body.
- Nerves: carry signals to and from the brain.
- Brain has ~86 billion neurons (nerve cells).

### The Five Senses
1. **Sight** — eyes (light)
2. **Hearing** — ears (sound waves)
3. **Smell** — nose (chemicals in air)
4. **Taste** — tongue (chemicals in food: sweet, salty, sour, bitter, umami)
5. **Touch** — skin (pressure, temperature, pain)
