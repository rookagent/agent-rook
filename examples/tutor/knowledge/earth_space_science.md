# Earth and Space Science

## The Solar System (Grades 3-5)

### The Sun
- A star — a giant ball of hot gas (hydrogen and helium)
- 93 million miles from Earth
- Provides light and heat that makes life possible
- About 1.3 million Earths could fit inside the Sun

### The Planets (in order from the Sun)
**My Very Enthusiastic Mother Just Served Us Nachos**
1. **Mercury** — Smallest, closest to Sun, no atmosphere, extreme temperatures
2. **Venus** — Hottest planet (thick atmosphere traps heat), rotates backwards, Earth's "twin" in size
3. **Earth** — Our home! Water, atmosphere, life. 1 moon.
4. **Mars** — The Red Planet. Has the largest volcano (Olympus Mons). 2 tiny moons.
5. **Jupiter** — Largest planet. Great Red Spot (giant storm). 95+ moons.
6. **Saturn** — Famous rings (ice and rock). 146+ moons. Could float in water (low density).
7. **Uranus** — Tilted sideways! Ice giant. Blue-green color from methane.
8. **Neptune** — Farthest planet. Strongest winds. Dark blue. Ice giant.

**Pluto:** Reclassified as a "dwarf planet" in 2006. Still cool though.

### Rocky vs. Gas
- **Rocky (terrestrial):** Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars — small, solid surface
- **Gas/Ice giants:** Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune — huge, no solid surface

### The Moon
- Earth's only natural satellite
- About 1/4 the size of Earth
- No atmosphere, no weather, no sound
- Moon phases: New → Crescent → Quarter → Gibbous → Full → and back
- One full cycle (new moon to new moon) = about 29.5 days
- The same side always faces Earth (tidally locked)
- Causes tides on Earth through gravity

## Day and Night, Seasons (Grades 3-5)

### Day and Night
Earth rotates (spins) on its axis once every 24 hours. The side facing the Sun has daytime. The side facing away has nighttime.

### Seasons
Earth is tilted 23.5° on its axis. As Earth orbits the Sun:
- **Summer:** Your hemisphere tilts TOWARD the Sun → more direct sunlight, longer days
- **Winter:** Your hemisphere tilts AWAY from the Sun → less direct sunlight, shorter days
- **Spring/Fall:** Transition periods

**Common misconception:** Seasons are NOT caused by Earth being closer/farther from the Sun. Earth is actually slightly closer in January (Northern Hemisphere winter)!

### Year
One full orbit around the Sun = 365.25 days. The extra .25 is why we have leap years every 4 years.

## Weather (Grades 2-4)

### What Causes Weather
The Sun heats the Earth unevenly (equator gets more than poles). This creates differences in air temperature and pressure, which creates wind, clouds, and precipitation.

### The Water Cycle
1. **Evaporation:** Sun heats water in oceans/lakes → water turns to invisible vapor → rises
2. **Condensation:** Water vapor cools as it rises → forms tiny droplets → clouds form
3. **Precipitation:** Droplets combine until too heavy → rain, snow, sleet, or hail
4. **Collection:** Water flows into streams, rivers, lakes, oceans → cycle repeats

### Cloud Types
- **Cumulus:** Fluffy, flat-bottomed cotton balls. Fair weather (usually).
- **Stratus:** Flat, gray, blanket-like layers. Overcast days, light rain.
- **Cirrus:** Thin, wispy, high up. Made of ice crystals. Fair weather but change may be coming.
- **Cumulonimbus:** Towering thunderstorm clouds. Dark, tall, dangerous. Lightning, heavy rain, hail, tornadoes.

### Severe Weather
- **Thunderstorm:** Warm air rises quickly, creates cumulonimbus clouds. Lightning = electrical discharge. Thunder = sound of lightning superheating air.
- **Tornado:** Spinning column of air from cloud to ground. Formed from severe thunderstorms. Fujita scale (EF0-EF5).
- **Hurricane:** Massive rotating storm over warm ocean water. Eye = calm center. Category 1-5.
- **Blizzard:** Heavy snow + strong winds + cold. Low visibility.

## Rocks and Minerals (Grades 3-5)

### Three Types of Rocks
1. **Igneous:** Formed from cooled magma/lava. "Fire rocks."
   - Granite (cooled slowly underground — large crystals)
   - Obsidian (cooled quickly above ground — glassy)
   - Basalt (cooled quickly — fine-grained, most common)

2. **Sedimentary:** Formed from layers of sediment pressed together over time.
   - Sandstone (compressed sand)
   - Limestone (compressed shells/coral)
   - Shale (compressed clay/mud)
   - Often contain fossils!

3. **Metamorphic:** Existing rocks changed by heat and pressure deep underground.
   - Marble (from limestone)
   - Slate (from shale)
   - Quartzite (from sandstone)

### The Rock Cycle
Any rock type can become any other:
- Igneous → weathering → sediment → Sedimentary
- Sedimentary → heat + pressure → Metamorphic
- Metamorphic → melting → magma → cools → Igneous
- Any rock → weathering → sediment → back around

### Minerals
- Natural, solid, non-living, specific chemical formula, crystal structure
- Over 4,000 known minerals
- Common: quartz, feldspar, mica, calcite
- Properties used to identify: hardness (Mohs scale 1-10), color, streak, luster, cleavage

## Earth's Structure (Grades 4-5)

### Layers (Outside In)
1. **Crust:** Thin outer shell. 5-70 km thick. Where we live.
2. **Mantle:** Thick, hot, slowly flowing rock. ~2,900 km thick.
3. **Outer core:** Liquid iron and nickel. Creates Earth's magnetic field. ~2,200 km thick.
4. **Inner core:** Solid iron and nickel. Extremely hot (~5,400°C). ~1,200 km radius.

**Explain it as:** "Like a hard-boiled egg — thin shell (crust), thick white (mantle), yolk (core)."

### Plate Tectonics (Grade 5)
Earth's crust is broken into large pieces (tectonic plates) that float on the mantle and slowly move.
- **Plates move apart:** New crust forms (mid-ocean ridges, rift valleys)
- **Plates collide:** Mountains form (Himalayas), one plate goes under another (subduction)
- **Plates slide past:** Earthquakes (San Andreas Fault)

### Earthquakes
- Caused by sudden movement along faults (cracks in Earth's crust)
- Measured on the Richter scale (each number is 10× more shaking)
- Focus = underground point of origin. Epicenter = surface point directly above.

### Volcanoes
- Openings in Earth's crust where magma (underground) becomes lava (surface)
- Found at plate boundaries and hot spots
- Types: shield (wide, gentle slope), composite (tall, explosive), cinder cone (small, steep)
