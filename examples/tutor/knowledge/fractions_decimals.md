# Fractions and Decimals

## Introducing Fractions (Grade 2-3)

### The Pizza Analogy
A fraction tells you how many pieces you have out of how many total.
- Cut a pizza into 4 equal slices. Eat 1. You ate 1/4 of the pizza.
- The bottom number (denominator) = how many total slices
- The top number (numerator) = how many you're talking about

### Key First Concepts
- **Equal parts matter** — if the slices aren't equal, it's not really 1/4
- **The whole can be anything** — 1/2 of a small pizza and 1/2 of a large pizza are different amounts
- **Fractions are numbers** — they live on the number line between whole numbers

### Familiar Fractions
- 1/2 = half (split something into 2 equal parts, take 1)
- 1/3 = one third (split into 3, take 1)
- 1/4 = one quarter (split into 4, take 1)
- 3/4 = three quarters (split into 4, take 3)

## Equivalent Fractions (Grade 3-4)

### The Big Idea
Different fractions can show the same amount: 1/2 = 2/4 = 3/6 = 4/8.

**Explain it as:** "If you cut each slice in half, you have twice as many slices but the same amount of pizza."

### Rule
Multiply (or divide) BOTH the top and bottom by the same number.
- 1/2 × 2/2 = 2/4 ✓
- 3/4 × 3/3 = 9/12 ✓
- 6/8 ÷ 2/2 = 3/4 ✓ (simplifying)

### Simplifying (Reducing)
Find the biggest number that divides into both the top and bottom evenly (GCF).
- 6/8: both divisible by 2 → 3/4
- 12/16: both divisible by 4 → 3/4
- 15/25: both divisible by 5 → 3/5

## Comparing Fractions (Grade 3-4)

### Same Denominator
Just compare the numerators: 3/8 vs 5/8 → 5/8 is bigger (more slices of the same size).

### Same Numerator
Compare denominators — SMALLER denominator = BIGGER fraction.
- 1/3 vs 1/5 → 1/3 is bigger (thirds are bigger slices than fifths)

### Different Everything
Find a common denominator, then compare:
- 2/3 vs 3/4 → 8/12 vs 9/12 → 3/4 is bigger

**Shortcut (butterfly method):** Cross multiply. 2×4=8 and 3×3=9. The bigger product points to the bigger fraction.

## Adding and Subtracting Fractions (Grade 4-5)

### Same Denominator (Easy)
Just add/subtract the numerators. Keep the denominator.
- 2/5 + 1/5 = 3/5
- 7/8 - 3/8 = 4/8 = 1/2

### Different Denominators
Find a common denominator first:
- 1/3 + 1/4: Common denominator = 12
- 4/12 + 3/12 = 7/12

**Steps:**
1. Find LCD (least common denominator)
2. Convert both fractions
3. Add or subtract numerators
4. Keep the denominator
5. Simplify if possible

## Multiplying Fractions (Grade 5)

### The Rule
Multiply straight across: numerator × numerator, denominator × denominator.
- 2/3 × 1/4 = 2/12 = 1/6

**Explain it as:** "1/4 of 2/3 of a pizza." You're taking a fraction OF a fraction — the result is smaller than either one.

### With Whole Numbers
Turn the whole number into a fraction: 3 × 2/5 = 3/1 × 2/5 = 6/5 = 1 1/5

## Dividing Fractions (Grade 5-6)

### Keep-Change-Flip
1. **Keep** the first fraction
2. **Change** division to multiplication
3. **Flip** the second fraction (reciprocal)

Example: 2/3 ÷ 1/4 = 2/3 × 4/1 = 8/3 = 2 2/3

**Explain it as:** "How many quarter-pizzas fit inside 2/3 of a pizza?"

## Mixed Numbers and Improper Fractions (Grade 4-5)

### Improper → Mixed
Divide numerator by denominator. Quotient = whole number, remainder = numerator.
- 7/4 = 1 whole pizza and 3/4 left = 1 3/4

### Mixed → Improper
Multiply whole number × denominator, add numerator.
- 2 1/3 = (2×3+1)/3 = 7/3

## Decimals (Grade 4-5)

### Place Values
- Ones . Tenths Hundredths Thousandths
- 3.47 = 3 ones + 4 tenths + 7 hundredths

**Explain it as:** "After the decimal point, each place is 10 times SMALLER."

### Fraction ↔ Decimal Conversion
- 1/2 = 0.5 (divide 1 by 2)
- 1/4 = 0.25
- 3/4 = 0.75
- 1/3 = 0.333... (repeating)
- 1/5 = 0.2
- 1/10 = 0.1

### Decimal ↔ Percent
Multiply by 100 (or move decimal point 2 places right):
- 0.5 = 50%
- 0.25 = 25%
- 0.1 = 10%
- 0.333... ≈ 33.3%

## Common Mistakes
1. **Adding denominators** — 1/3 + 1/4 ≠ 2/7! Must find common denominator first.
2. **Bigger denominator = bigger fraction** — nope, it's the opposite. 1/3 > 1/5.
3. **Forgetting to simplify** — 4/8 is correct but 1/2 is the expected answer.
4. **Mixed number operations** — convert to improper fractions first, then operate.
