# Geometry

## 2D Shapes (Grades K-3)

### Basic Shapes
| Shape | Sides | Angles | Real-World Example |
|-------|-------|--------|-------------------|
| Circle | 0 | 0 | Pizza, clock, wheel |
| Triangle | 3 | 3 | Yield sign, pizza slice, roof |
| Square | 4 (equal) | 4 (right) | Window, checkerboard square, tile |
| Rectangle | 4 (opposite equal) | 4 (right) | Door, phone, book |
| Pentagon | 5 | 5 | Home plate in baseball |
| Hexagon | 6 | 6 | Honeycomb cell, stop sign-ish |
| Octagon | 8 | 8 | Stop sign |

### Triangles by Sides
- **Equilateral:** All 3 sides equal, all angles 60°
- **Isosceles:** 2 sides equal, 2 angles equal
- **Scalene:** No equal sides, no equal angles

### Triangles by Angles
- **Right triangle:** One 90° angle (square corner)
- **Acute triangle:** All angles less than 90°
- **Obtuse triangle:** One angle greater than 90°

### Quadrilaterals Family
- **Square:** 4 equal sides, 4 right angles
- **Rectangle:** Opposite sides equal, 4 right angles
- **Rhombus:** 4 equal sides, angles NOT necessarily 90°
- **Parallelogram:** Opposite sides equal and parallel
- **Trapezoid:** Exactly one pair of parallel sides

**Explain it as:** "A square is a special rectangle (all sides equal). A rectangle is a special parallelogram (all right angles). Shapes can be part of a family!"

## Perimeter (Grades 3-4)

### What It Is
The distance AROUND a shape. Like walking along the fence of a yard.

### How to Find It
Add up all the sides:
- Rectangle: P = 2 × length + 2 × width (or 2 × (L + W))
- Square: P = 4 × side
- Triangle: P = side₁ + side₂ + side₃
- Any shape: Add all sides

### Real-World Applications
- How much fencing for a garden?
- How much ribbon to go around a picture frame?
- How far is it to walk around the playground?

## Area (Grades 3-5)

### What It Is
How much space a shape covers. Like how many floor tiles you'd need.

### Formulas
| Shape | Area Formula | Remember It As |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| Rectangle | A = length × width | Rows × columns of tiles |
| Square | A = side × side (side²) | Same as rectangle but all sides equal |
| Triangle | A = 1/2 × base × height | Half of a rectangle |
| Parallelogram | A = base × height | Slide it into a rectangle |
| Circle | A = π × radius² | ~3.14 × r × r |

### Units
Area is measured in SQUARE units: square inches (in²), square feet (ft²), square meters (m²), square centimeters (cm²).

**Explain it as:** "Perimeter is like the fence — measured in regular units. Area is like the grass inside — measured in square units because you're covering a flat space."

## Angles (Grades 4-5)

### What's an Angle?
Two lines meeting at a point. Measured in degrees (°). A full turn = 360°.

### Types
- **Right angle:** Exactly 90° — like the corner of a book. Marked with a small square.
- **Acute angle:** Less than 90° — "a cute little angle"
- **Obtuse angle:** More than 90° but less than 180° — "an obese angle" (wider)
- **Straight angle:** Exactly 180° — a flat line

### Measuring with a Protractor
1. Place the center point on the vertex (corner) of the angle
2. Line up one side of the angle with 0°
3. Read where the other side crosses the scale
4. Use the correct scale (inner or outer — one starts at 0° from the left, one from the right)

### Triangle Angle Rule
All three angles in ANY triangle add up to 180°. Always.
- Equilateral: 60° + 60° + 60° = 180°
- Right triangle: 90° + 45° + 45° = 180° (isosceles right)

## Symmetry (Grades 3-4)

### Line Symmetry
A line of symmetry divides a shape into two identical halves (mirror images).
- Square: 4 lines of symmetry
- Rectangle: 2 lines of symmetry
- Equilateral triangle: 3 lines of symmetry
- Circle: Infinite lines of symmetry
- The letter A: 1 line of symmetry
- The letter B: 1 line of symmetry (horizontal)

### Real-World Symmetry
Butterflies, faces (approximately), snowflakes, hearts, the alphabet (some letters).

## 3D Shapes (Grades 2-5)

### Solids
| Shape | Faces | Edges | Vertices | Real-World |
|-------|-------|-------|----------|-----------|
| Cube | 6 squares | 12 | 8 | Dice, Rubik's cube |
| Rectangular prism | 6 rectangles | 12 | 8 | Shoe box, brick |
| Sphere | 0 (curved) | 0 | 0 | Ball, globe |
| Cylinder | 2 circles + curved | 2 | 0 | Can, paper towel roll |
| Cone | 1 circle + curved | 1 | 1 | Ice cream cone, party hat |
| Pyramid | Triangles + base | Varies | Varies | Egyptian pyramid |

### Volume (Grade 5)
How much space a 3D shape takes up. Like how much water it could hold.
- Rectangular prism: V = length × width × height
- Cube: V = side³
- Measured in CUBIC units: in³, ft³, cm³, m³

**Explain it as:** "Area is like painting a wall (flat). Volume is like filling a fish tank (3D space)."

## Coordinate Geometry (Grade 5)

### The Coordinate Plane
- Two number lines crossing at (0,0) called the origin
- Horizontal = x-axis, Vertical = y-axis
- A point is (x, y): go RIGHT x units, then UP y units
- (3, 5) means: right 3, up 5

**Remember:** "You run before you jump" — x (horizontal) first, y (vertical) second.
