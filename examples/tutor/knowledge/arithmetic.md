# Arithmetic

## Teaching Addition (Grades K-2)

### Concept Progression
1. **Counting objects** — put 3 blocks and 2 blocks together, count all of them
2. **Counting on** — start at 3, count up 2 more: "3... 4, 5!"
3. **Number bonds** — 5 is made of 3 and 2, or 4 and 1, or 5 and 0
4. **Addition facts** — memorize sums to 20
5. **Column addition** — ones first, then tens, then hundreds

### Regrouping (Carrying)
When the ones column adds up to 10 or more, carry the tens digit.
Example: 47 + 35
- Ones: 7 + 5 = 12. Write 2, carry 1.
- Tens: 4 + 3 + 1 (carried) = 8.
- Answer: 82.

**Explain it as:** "The ones column got too full — more than 9 — so we send the extra ten over to the tens column."

### Common Mistakes
- Forgetting to carry
- Carrying the wrong digit (carrying 2 instead of 1 from 12)
- Misaligning columns

## Teaching Subtraction (Grades K-3)

### Concept Progression
1. **Taking away** — 5 cookies, eat 2, how many left?
2. **Counting back** — start at 7, count back 3: "7... 6, 5, 4!"
3. **Difference** — how much MORE is 8 than 5? (thinking of distance on a number line)
4. **Subtraction facts** — memorize differences within 20
5. **Column subtraction with regrouping**

### Regrouping (Borrowing)
When the top digit is smaller than the bottom digit, borrow from the next column.
Example: 52 - 27
- Ones: 2 - 7? Can't do it. Borrow from tens: 12 - 7 = 5.
- Tens: 4 (was 5, borrowed 1) - 2 = 2.
- Answer: 25.

**Explain it as:** "We need more ones, so we break open a ten — one ten becomes ten ones."

## Teaching Multiplication (Grades 2-4)

### Concept Progression
1. **Repeated addition** — 3 × 4 means 4 + 4 + 4 (three groups of four)
2. **Arrays** — 3 rows of 4 dots = 12 dots total
3. **Skip counting** — count by 3s: 3, 6, 9, 12, 15...
4. **Times tables** — memorize through 12 × 12
5. **Multi-digit multiplication**

### Times Tables Strategies
- **×2:** Double it (same as adding the number to itself)
- **×3:** Double, then add one more group
- **×4:** Double twice (4 × 6 = 2 × 6 = 12, then 12 × 2 = 24)
- **×5:** Count by 5s (end in 0 or 5)
- **×9:** Fingers trick — hold up 10 fingers, put down the Nth finger, left side = tens, right side = ones
- **×10:** Add a zero
- **×11:** For single digits, repeat the digit (11 × 7 = 77)

### Multi-Digit Multiplication
Break it into steps:
- 34 × 7: (30 × 7) + (4 × 7) = 210 + 28 = 238
- Or use the standard algorithm: multiply ones, carry, multiply tens, add.

### Common Mistakes
- Forgetting to add the carried number
- Misplacing the zero when multiplying by the tens digit
- Confusing ×0 (always 0) and ×1 (always the number)

## Teaching Division (Grades 3-5)

### Concept Progression
1. **Sharing equally** — 12 cookies for 4 kids = how many each?
2. **Grouping** — how many groups of 3 in 12?
3. **Inverse of multiplication** — 12 ÷ 4 = ? means 4 × ? = 12
4. **Division facts** — use times tables in reverse
5. **Long division**

### Long Division Steps (Dad, Mom, Sister, Brother)
1. **D**ivide — how many times does the divisor go into the first digit(s)?
2. **M**ultiply — divisor × quotient digit
3. **S**ubtract — subtract the product
4. **B**ring down — bring down the next digit
5. Repeat until no digits left.

### Remainders
Sometimes it doesn't divide evenly. 14 ÷ 3 = 4 remainder 2.
**Explain it as:** "We made 4 full groups of 3, but had 2 left over that couldn't make another group."

## Word Problems Strategy (All Operations)

Teach the CUBES method:
- **C**ircle the numbers
- **U**nderline the question (what are they asking?)
- **B**ox the key words (total, difference, each, per, left, altogether)
- **E**valuate — which operation? (together = add, take away = subtract, groups of = multiply, share = divide)
- **S**olve and check

### Key Words
| Word | Usually Means |
|------|--------------|
| Total, altogether, sum, in all | Addition |
| Difference, left, fewer, how many more | Subtraction |
| Each, per, every, groups of, times | Multiplication |
| Share, split, divide, per person | Division |

**Warning:** Key words aren't foolproof. "How many more" can be subtraction OR addition depending on context. Teach kids to THINK about what's happening, not just scan for words.
