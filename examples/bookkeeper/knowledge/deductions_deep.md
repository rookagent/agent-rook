# Tax Deductions — Deep Reference for Small Business

## The Golden Rule of Deductions
An expense is deductible if it is **ordinary** (common in your industry) AND **necessary** (helpful and appropriate for your business). IRS Publication 535, Chapter 1.

If audited, YOU prove the deduction. Keep receipts, invoices, bank statements, and a contemporaneous log for vehicle and meals.

## Home Office Deduction (Form 8829 or Simplified Method)

### Qualification
The space must be used **regularly and exclusively** for business. A kitchen table where you also eat dinner does NOT qualify. A dedicated room or partitioned area does.

Two exceptions:
1. **Daycare facilities** — can be used for non-business purposes during non-business hours
2. **Storage of inventory** — if your home is the only fixed location of your business

### Method 1: Simplified ($5/sq ft, max 300 sq ft = $1,500)
Easy, no depreciation recapture risk. Just multiply square footage by $5. Max deduction: $1,500/year. Report directly on Schedule C Line 30.

### Method 2: Actual Expenses (Form 8829)
Calculate the percentage of your home used for business (square footage of office / total home square footage). Apply that percentage to:
- Mortgage interest or rent
- Property taxes
- Utilities (electric, gas, water, internet)
- Homeowner's insurance
- Repairs (whole-house repairs prorated, office-only repairs 100%)
- Depreciation of the home (residential property, 39 years)

**Warning:** Actual method triggers depreciation recapture when you sell the home. The simplified method does not.

### Both Methods
- Can still deduct direct expenses for the office (painting the office, dedicated phone line) 100%
- Home office establishes your home as principal place of business, making commute miles to client sites deductible

## Vehicle Deduction

### Standard Mileage Rate
- 2024: 67 cents per mile
- 2025: Check IRS announcement (usually published in December)
- Must use this method in the first year the vehicle is used for business to preserve the option
- Track every trip: date, destination, business purpose, miles
- Apps: MileIQ, Everlance, or a paper log

### Actual Expense Method
Track ALL vehicle costs: gas, oil, tires, insurance, registration, repairs, car washes, loan interest, lease payments, depreciation.
- Multiply total costs by business use percentage
- Business use % = business miles / total miles
- Must maintain a mileage log either way

### Which Method Is Better?
- **Standard mileage** usually wins for fuel-efficient cars, older cars, or cars with low maintenance costs
- **Actual expenses** usually wins for expensive vehicles, gas guzzlers, or vehicles with high repair costs
- **You choose** in Year 1 and can switch in subsequent years (with restrictions if you used actual first)

### What Counts as Business Miles
- Client sites, job sites, meetings, bank, post office, office supply store
- Home to temporary work location (if home is your principal place of business)
- Between two business locations in the same day

### What Does NOT Count
- Commuting (home to regular office) — NEVER deductible
- Personal errands, even if done during the workday
- Moving violations or parking tickets

## Meals and Entertainment

### Meals (50% Deductible)
- Business meals with clients, prospects, or associates where business is discussed
- Meals while traveling overnight for business
- **Must document:** date, place, amount, who was there, business purpose
- Lavish or extravagant meals may be disallowed

### Entertainment (0% Deductible since 2018)
- Golf, concerts, sporting events, theater — NOT deductible even if business is discussed
- Exception: meals at entertainment events if separately invoiced/stated

### Employee Meals (Various)
- Holiday party or company picnic: 100% deductible
- Meals for employee convenience on premises: 50% deductible
- De minimis meals (coffee, snacks in office): 100% deductible

## Travel Expenses

### Deductible Travel
Must be overnight and away from your "tax home" (principal place of business):
- Airfare (coach or business class)
- Hotel/lodging
- Taxi, Uber, rental car
- Tips (bellhop, housekeeping, taxi)
- Dry cleaning/laundry while traveling
- Business calls, internet, fax
- Baggage fees
- 50% of meals while traveling

### Mixed Business/Personal Trips
- **Domestic:** If the trip is primarily business, travel costs (airfare, transport) are fully deductible. Only deduct expenses for business days.
- **International:** If more than 25% of days are personal, you must prorate airfare. Weekends and holidays between business days count as business days.
- **Spouse travel:** NOT deductible unless spouse is an employee with a bona fide business purpose

## Equipment and Technology

### De Minimis Safe Harbor (Reg. 1.263(a)-1(f))
Items under $2,500 per invoice (or per item) can be expensed immediately rather than depreciated. You must have a written accounting policy in place and apply it consistently.
- Applies to: laptops, monitors, phones, printers, tools, small equipment
- Elect annually on your tax return

### Section 179 (Form 4562)
Immediate expensing of qualifying business assets:
- 2024 limit: $1,220,000
- Phase-out begins at $3,050,000 in total purchases
- Qualifying: tangible personal property, off-the-shelf software, certain improvements
- NOT qualifying: real property (buildings), land, inventory
- Must be placed in service during the tax year
- Cannot create a loss — limited to business income

### Bonus Depreciation
- 2024: 60% (phasing down from 100% in 2022)
- 2025: 40%
- 2026: 20%
- 2027: 0%
- Applies to NEW and USED property (changed by TCJA)
- CAN create a loss (unlike Section 179)

## Insurance Premiums

### Self-Employed Health Insurance (1040 Line 17, not Schedule C)
- Deduct 100% of health, dental, and vision premiums for yourself, spouse, and dependents
- Only if you're not eligible for employer-sponsored coverage from another job
- Cannot exceed net self-employment income
- This is an "above the line" deduction — reduces AGI

### Business Insurance (Schedule C Line 15)
- General liability, professional liability, workers' comp, business property
- Cyber liability, product liability, errors & omissions
- Key person life insurance (if business is the beneficiary)

## Education and Professional Development

### Deductible
- Courses, seminars, workshops that maintain or improve skills in your CURRENT business
- Professional certifications and continuing education
- Industry conferences (registration, travel, lodging)
- Trade publications, professional subscriptions
- Books directly related to your business

### NOT Deductible
- Education to qualify for a NEW trade or business (law school, medical school)
- Education to meet minimum requirements for your current job
- Personal enrichment courses

## Software and Subscriptions

### Fully Deductible (Schedule C Line 27a: Other Expenses)
- Accounting software (QuickBooks, FreshBooks, Wave)
- Industry-specific software
- Cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox, iCloud — business portion)
- Email/communication (Google Workspace, Microsoft 365)
- Website hosting, domain names
- Design tools (Adobe Creative Cloud, Canva Pro)
- Project management (Asana, Monday, Notion)
- CRM software
- AI tools used for business

### Partially Deductible (Prorate personal vs business use)
- Phone plan (business % of monthly bill)
- Internet (business % — usually based on home office %)
- Streaming services (only if genuinely used for business, e.g., background music in a retail store)

## Retirement Contributions

### SEP-IRA
- Contribute up to 25% of net self-employment income (after SE tax deduction)
- Max: $69,000 (2024)
- No employee contributions (employer-only)
- Easy to set up, deadline: tax filing date + extensions
- Deducted on 1040 Schedule 1, Line 16

### Solo 401(k)
- Employee contribution: $23,000 (2024) + $7,500 catch-up if 50+
- Employer contribution: up to 25% of net SE income
- Total max: $69,000 (2024) + catch-up
- Can do Roth contributions on employee side
- Must be established by December 31, contributions by tax filing deadline

### SIMPLE IRA
- Employee contribution: $16,000 (2024) + $3,500 catch-up if 50+
- Employer match: 2% or 3% of compensation
- Lower limits but simpler administration
- Must be established by October 1

## Documentation Requirements

For EVERY deduction, maintain:
1. **What** — description of expense
2. **How much** — amount (receipt)
3. **When** — date of expense
4. **Where** — place of business/travel (for meals and travel)
5. **Why** — business purpose
6. **Who** — who was present (for meals)

Keep records for **at least 3 years** from filing date (6 years if income is understated by >25%, indefinitely for fraud or non-filing).

Digital records (photos of receipts, bank statements, accounting software exports) are accepted by the IRS.
